3D Modeling

As the Animation Lead and Costume Designer for my Vertically Integrated Project, CineInnovate Studios, I was responsible for managing a team of designers and creating the costumes for our characters. For this process, I began by using Procreate to sketch the initial designs, then modeled the costumes in Marvelous Designer. Afterward, I refined the models in Blender, adding final touch-ups and texturing. The shoes and clothing were sourced from free assets on Sketchfab.

Here are two characters I worked on:

Moto

Moto, the main character, required a vibrant outfit that matched his eccentric personality. After deciding on the pattern for his sweatshirt, I created the design and textures on the UV unwrap in Procreate. Once the outfit was modeled in Marvelous Designer, I moved to Blender to apply the UV wrap and fine-tune the details.

David

For David, I modeled the beanie in Blender, while the rest of his outfit was pieced together in Marvelous Designer. Similar to Moto, I created the patterns on a UV unwrap in Procreate and applied the texture in Blender.
